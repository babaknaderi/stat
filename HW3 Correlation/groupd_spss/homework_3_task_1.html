Example

Calculate the suitable correlation coefficients for all speeker characteristics (unsympatisch to bescheiden) with each other and report which collerations are significant.

Dataset: likeability_dimension_ratings.csv

Setup and transfering data format

To make sure the data is usable and no outliers affect the correlations later, follow these steps:

  1. Simply load the dataset into SPSS
  2. Analyze → Descriptive Statistics → Explore
  3. Add all characteristics to „Dependent List“-Field
  4. Check „Plots“ in the „Display“-area
  5. Click „Plots...“, unselect „Stem and leaf“ and close the window
  6. Click „Ok“

In the boxplot you can see that all values are between 0 and 100, as it should be. While two values at the top are outside of the four quartiles and could qualify as outliers, they are not far away from the border and still within the 0 to 100 range.

Performing the test

  1. Analyze → Correlate → Bivariate
  2. Add all characteistics to the „Variables“-field, check „Pearson“ in the „Correlation Coefficients“-area and set „Test of Significance“ to „Two-tailed“ and check „Flag significant correlations“
  3. Style the output:
    1. Click „Style...“
    2. Change „Value“ to „Significance“, „Condition“ to „x>0.05“ and the background color to grey → As most of the output will have a high significance, we will only mark the correlations with a low significance.
    3. Click „Add“ → In the new condition change „Value“ to „Correlation“, „Condition“ to „Abs(x)>=0.5“ and give it a flashy color → The pairs with a high correlation will be highlighted in the output.
    4. Click „Continue“ to close the window
  4. Click „Ok“ to generate the output

Output

Correlations higher than 0,5 are marked yellow. Significances higher than 0,05 are marked grey and should not be considered
