Example

Calculate the suitable correlation coefficients for the difference between speeker and listener age with the characteristic “attraktiv”

Dataset: likeability_dimension_ratings.csv

Setup and transfering data format

Load the dataset into SPSS. Make sure to select the delimiter as “comma”>, decimal symbol as “period” and text qualifier as “none” and check “First line contains variable names”

Performing the test

Click on Transform → Compute variable

Write “Age_Difference” as Target Variable and “speaker_age - listener_age”as Numeric Expression to get the difference between the listener’s age and the speaker’s age.

Make the boxplots for detecting the outliers. Analyze → Descriptive Statistics → Explore

Add “Age_Difference” and “Attraktiv” to „Dependent List“-Field. Check „Plots“ in the „Display“-area. Click „Plots...“, unselect „Stem and leaf“ and close the window. Click „Ok“

The boxplots show no outliers for “attrackiv” but many for “Age_Difference”.

To find the upper and lower limit in the boxplot, double-click the boxplot → Click on “Y” → Change the “Major Increment” to 1 in the “Scale” tab and select “Major Ticks Only” in the “Grid Lines” tab.

From the output the upper limit is 15 and the lower limit is -12 excluding the outliers

To remove the outliers from the data, “Data” → ”Select Cases”

Select “If Condition is Satisfied” and “Delete Unselected Cases” and then, click on “If…”.

Write “((Age_Difference <=15) AND (Age_Difference >= -12))” expression and click continue and “OK”.

The scatter plot doesn’t show a linear relationship between “Age_Difference” and “Attrakiv”. So, Spearmen correlation is suitable for this task.

To calculate correlation choose Analyze → Correlate → Bivariate

Add “Age_Difference” and “Attraktiv” in Variables and check “Spearman” and click “OK”
