Example

Does the test environment (independent variable), i.e. Lab (ID=1) or home (ID=0), have a significant influence on speech quality ratings (dependent variable)?

Use the quality ratings of condition 3 provided in the file (lab_crowd_speech_quality).

Setup and transfering data format

Test Requirements

Test assumptions:

  • There should not be any significant outliers in the dataset.
  • Dependent variable should be approximately normally distributed for each group of the independent variable.
  • Homogeneity of variances: in SPSS, when conducting the independent T-Test, the Levene’s test will be computed as well. Based on the outcome of the homogeneity test, you will have to decide, which outcome of the t-test to interpret.

Analyze > Descriptive Statistics > Explore… > Add “Quality” to “Dependent List” and “Environment” to the “Factor List”

Click on “Statistics…” button > In the “Statistics” modal, check the option “Outliers”.

Click on “Plots…” button > In the “Plots” modal check the option “Normality plots with tests” and under the “Descriptive”-section uncheck “Stem-and-leaf” and check “Histogram”.

Independent-Samples T Test

Conduct the independent t-test.

Analyze > Compare Means > Independent-Samples T Test… > Add “Quality” to “Test Variable(s)” list and “Environment” as the “Grouping Variable”. > Click on “Define Groups...” > For “Group 1” enter the value of the “home” environment type (0) and for “Group 2” the one of the “lab” environment type (1).

Output interpretation

For the "home" condition, there are four outliers (ID 206, 208, 209, 211) marked in the boxplot.
To check if the outliers affect the data, we compare the 5% trimmed mean and mean values. If there is a significant difference, the outliers should be removed. In this case, there is only a minimal difference between the values, implying that the presence of the outliers does not influence the outcomes of the tests.

The Shapiro-Wilk test showed a significant departure from normality in both the “home” (W(213)=.884, p=.000) and “lab” (W(192)=.860, p=.000) conditions.

An independent-samples t-test was conducted to compare the speech quality ratings in home and lab conditions. Levene’s test for equality of variances indicated homogeneity of variances, F = .09, p = .764. There was a significant difference in the ratings for home (M= 3.12, SD= 0.858) and for lab (M= 2.83, SD= 0.829) conditions; t(403)=3.44, p=0.001, d=-0,34. Persons performing the rating process at home assessed higher ratings to the test material compared to persons performing the test in the lab.

To measure the effect size, we compute Cohen's D. SPSS does not provide any function for the effect size, but this can be computed based on the following formula:

We can read the means and standard deviations from the output of the independent t-test.

d = (2,83-3,12)/sqrt((0,858^2 + 0,829^2)/2)= -0,29/sqrt((0,74 + 0,69)/2)= -0,29/sqrt(0,715)= -0,29/0,85 = -0,34

Cohen’s effect size value (d =-0.34) suggests low practical significance.
