Example

Does the testStimulus have a significant influence on speech quality ratings and if yes, for which cases. Please assume that each of the six files was assessed by a different set of test participants. Use the quality ratings provided in the data set speech_quality_repetition_dataset.

Setup and transfering data format

ANOVA

Conduct the test.

Analyze > Compare Means > One-Way ANOVA...

In the One-Way ANOVA modal, add the rating variable to the Dependent List and testStimulus to the Factor section.

In the Options modal, under the Statistics section check Descriptives, Homogenity of variance test, Welch options.

In the Post Hoc modal, choose to perform a Tukey and a Games-Howell tests since we do not know if the assumpition of variance homogenity is given.

Output interpretation

Leveneâ€™s test of homogenity of variances indicated unequal variances (F = 7,80, p < .05) among the data. As a consequence, computing an ANOVA would result in erronated values. An alternative would be to compute a Welch-ANOVA, test which does not assume equality of variances.


In the case of a Welch-ANOVA, results are to be read from the Robust Test of Equality of Means table.

Interpretation: The type of test stimulus has a significant influence on the speech quality ratings, Welch's F(5, 410,46) = 326,08, p < .05.


In the case of variance heterogenity, the Games-Howell post-hoc test is the alternative to the Tukey post-hoc test. Consequently, the results of the post-hoc tests, are to be read from the line indicated by the Games-Howell label.

Games-Howell post-hoc analysis revealed a significant difference (p < .001) between the speech quality ratings of almost all stimulus types/ files. An exception is to be made in the cases of the 1st and the 4th (p = 1.00), the 2nd and the 5th (p = .82), and the 3rd and 6th (p = 0.73) stimulus type, where no significant difference was recognized between the speech quality ratings.
